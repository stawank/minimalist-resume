Overview

During my undergraduate years, I was part of Team Aryans at Government College of Engineering, Aurangabad — our university's Baja SAE competition team. Baja SAE challenges student engineers to design, fabricate, and race a single-seat off-road vehicle powered by a fixed Briggs & Stratton 10HP engine, making the drivetrain and CVT the primary competitive differentiator.

Over three seasons I grew from team member to CVT & Transmission Head, responsible for the complete powertrain design — culminating in All India Rank 2 at Baja SAE India 2018 and an international appearance at Baja SAE Oregon 2018 in Portland, OR.

CVT & Drivetrain Work
Customised CVT Transmission
Designed and tuned a CVT system tailored to the vehicle's event profile. With the engine locked by regulation, the CVT is the critical performance lever — governing acceleration, hill-climb torque, and endurance reliability.
Static Speed Reduction Gearbox
Engineered a gearbox stage to achieve the target final drive ratio, balancing top speed against low-end torque for varied off-road terrain conditions.
Custom Axle Development
Designed and fabricated bespoke axles sized to the vehicle's specific load cases and packaging constraints — built to survive the sustained stresses of 4-hour endurance races.
